William Ruto's camp is attracting leaders with criminal tendencies, Otiende Amollo

Rarieda MP Otiende Amollo has alleged Deputy President William Ruto's Tanga Tanga wing has an alarming tendency of attracting leaders with criminal records.

Otiende who was reacting to the increasing incidents of political interference in the country said the DP's camp has a fair share of the blame to take home for assembling a team of politicians with questionable characters.

Speaking on Citizen TV on Thursday, February 4, the Rarieda lawmaker said there were valid reasons why Ruto's camp has been on the radar of the law enforcing agencies for a while.

"William Samoei's (Ruto) side seems to attract a disproportionate number of leaders with criminal tendencies. Look at the Standard (newspaper) only, page 2, page 6, page 18, page 20. All those pages have leaders who are affiliated to William Ruto's side and with criminal issues, so if that is the case, you don't blame the those who are trying to enforce the law," said Otiende.

The ODM lawmaker also took issue with the hustler narrative being peddled by the DP's camp.

While supporting the recommendation by the National Assembly's security committee that the word should be criminalised, Otiende argued the dictionary definition of a hustler alone was enough reason to make a team identifying itself with it look like criminals.

"The dictionary definition of a hustler is a person who obtains by deceitful or illicit means, a person who practices theft or swindling, all those are things are already criminal, you don't need to criminalise the word because the what and the act is criminal...what we don't want is to create a class war that will actually destabilise everything that we know as a nation," said the senior counsel.
