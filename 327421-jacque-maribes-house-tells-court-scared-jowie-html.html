Jacque Maribe's house help tells court she was scared of Jowie

July 02, 2024
Post

- Pamela Kembo was employed by Jacque Maribe to work as house help in 2016

- In 2018, Jowi moved into live with Maribe and Kembo was told he was a soldier working in Dubai

- Kembo told the court Jowi never liked her and at one point he accused her of stealing money

- According to her, after Monicas Kimani's murder Jowi shot himself on the shoulder in the bedroom

Jacque Maribe's house help has told the High Court in Nairobi that she was always scared of Joseph Irungi alias Jowi because he walked around with a gun in his pocket.

Appearing in court on Thursday, November 28, Pamela Kembo who was employed by Maribe in 2016 said she Jowi was introduced to her as a soldier who works in Dubai but neighbours knew he worked at state house.

Kembo told Justice James Wakiaga that the main suspect in murder of Monica Kimani moved into Maribe's Lang'ata house in 2018 and he never liked her.

“Every time, Jowie would walk around with a gun in his pocket. As a result, I was extremely scared of him,” said Kembo.

She said at one point he even accused her of stealing money meant for the house rent.

“He often got angry at me, and would scold me often. At one point, he accused me of stealing money meant for house rent,” she said.

The house help said Jowi would always wake up early in the morning and drive the journalist to her former workplace at Citizen TV before returning home to sleep.

On the fateful day that Monica was killed, Kembo told the court that Jowi left the house at around 4pm driving in his lover's car but did not return until the following day.

“On Wednesday, September 19, 2018, one of the two days (19 and 20) that businesswoman Monica Kimani was killed, Jowie put on a brown pair of shorts, white T-shirt, sneakers, and a cap. He, thereafter, left in Maribe’s car carrying a bag. That was 4pm,"

He returned to the house on Thursday afternoon and drove Maribe to work at around 4pm.

After dropping her, he returned to the house but left shortly after wearing a grey pair of shorts.

“He returned to the house deep in the night, but I did not know until 1am Friday, September 21, when I heard him screaming in pain, and calling the name of one Brian Kasaine, a neighbour who was his friend,"

When Kasaine responded Jowi was bleeding profusely from the shoulder at the time and he helped Maribe carry him out of the house.

The three left together but she had no idea where they went to.

“I, thereafter, went back into the house to check if Maribe’s son was okay. In Maribe’s bedroom, I found a gun on the floor and blood spots strewn all over,”

Amid all the drama and confusion, Kembo called her former employer and Maribe's friend Terryanne Chebet and informed her that Jowi had shot himself on the shoulder.

“Jowie, Kasaine and Maribe returned home at 4am Friday, September 21. Kasaine left for his house, whereas Jowie and Maribe locked themselves in their bedroom.”

On Saturday, September 22, Kembo requested for time off duty and left Maribe's to spend the night at Chebet’s house in Kilimani.

The house help told the court she returned to Maribe's house on Sunday and Jowi was brought back home on Monday by Kasaine.

“On Tuesday, September 25, 2018, police officers arrived at Maribe’s house at around 6:30am. They went straight to her bedroom, combed the room for any evidence, and left with Jowie. That day, Maribe left for work in a taxi. I hadn’t seen her vehicle around,"

She later received a call from Maribe's brother who asked her to prepare the boy so that they go to their home in Buru Buru.

Kembo left the house with the boy and spent the night in Buru Buru but on the following day she requested Maribe’s mum to give her bus fare so that she could travel to their rural home in Kitale, Trans-Nzoia county.

“Terryanne Chebet called and told me to ‘relax’. She asked me to go and spend over at her place in Kilimani.”
