Nairobi workers in the entertainment and hospitality industry have asked the government to lift the 8pm curfew in the five counties.

The group held a peaceful match in Nairobi's Central Business District on Thursday, March 31, during which calls to unlock the country were made.

The event's organiser Eric Maway who spoke TUKO.co.ke said most of those working in the entertainment and hospitality sector lost their jobs just when they were beginning to pick up.

"We plead with the president to unlock the country and open restaurants, bars and lift the 8pm curfew. Thousands of people have lost their jobs including me! Waiters, waitresses, bouncers, DJs, and musicians of this country have rent to pay and we have kids to look after," he told TUKO.co.ke.

Eric, who has also been rendered jobless thanks to President Uhuru Kenyatta's public order issued on Friday, March 26, said the only gift the head of state should give them was opening up the economy.

"We need to go back to work we can fight this pandemic by observing social distance and other COVID-19 protocol but not hunger. I have been receiving calls from people I don't know they threatening me for posting and talking about this," he added.

The protests began at the Nation Centre before matching to the Kenya Cinema and Freedom Corner and later converged at the Harambee House Office of the President.

On Friday, March 26, the head of state declared a cessation of movement in and out of Kiambu, Nakuru, Nairobi, Kajiado and Machakos counties which he classified as coronavirus disease infected areas.

Additionally, the president ordered the prohibition of alcohol sale in the five counties, while restaurants and eateries in the said counties shall provide takeaway services only.

Physical learning in educational institutions, including universities and tertiary and vocational colleges, was also suspended.
