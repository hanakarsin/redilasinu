Census 2019: Wajir chiefs arrested for inflating figures

March 10, 2024
Post

- The three were were arrested after investigations revealed they were involved in a malpractice

- This was after the number of dwellers in some households tripled days after prelisting

- Police are looking for more culprits

Police in Wajir South on Monday, August 26, arrested two chiefs for facilitating inflation of census figures through double listing of households.

The Khumbi and Habaswein central location chiefs are reported to have been overseeing the registration of people twice in a move aimed at exaggerating the final outcome.

According to Capital FM, National Police Service Charles Owino confirmed the chiefs' arrest noting they will be charged in court on Tuesday, August 27.

“This is a serious offence. The chief is in custody and will be charged," Owino said.

The chiefs were identified as Musa Abdi (Khumbi location) and Khalif Sirat Farah (Habaswein central) and were arrested alongside Habaswein central assistant chief Harriet Maow.

According to Wajir County Commissioner Loyford Kibaara, more chiefs in the county were being sought over the offence.

"We are holding two chiefs and an assistant chief for allegedly inflating household units in their areas, investigations have been launched,” Kibaara said.“We are looking for more chiefs after we received information that they had engaged in the offence across the county,” he added.

He said they noticed that the number of households in their respective areas had increased tremendously compared to what was captured during mapping and pre-listing.

Other areas under surveillance are Mandera West and North and parts of Lafey sub county.

Authorities believe the move is part of a scheme by politicians to inflate population figures in their regions for political gains especially in areas that are sparsely populated.
