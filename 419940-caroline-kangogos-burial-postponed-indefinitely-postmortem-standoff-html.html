Caroline Kangogo's burial postponed indefinitely after postmortem standoff

March 31, 2024
Post
  • The burial of Caroline Kangogo has been postponed after the planned autopsy that was scheduled for Friday, July 23, failed to take place
  • The government's chief pathologist Johansen Oduor claimed he was alerted late about the autopsy
  • According to the family, the DCI had asked them to prepare for burial a day after the autopsy
  • A stand-off ensued at the funeral home after the family attempted to storm inside to forcefully remove the body

Caroline Kangogo, the suspected police killer, may not be buried today, Saturday, because the planned autopsy failed to take place on Friday, July 23.

The autopsy was set to occur at the Iten County Referral Hospital morgue, but that did not happen.

Her parents, relatives, and friends were still camping out at the funeral home, appealing for the body to be released for burial, The Standard reported.

The family further accused the DCI of the failed autopsy, claiming the state agency had allowed them to bury their daughter on Saturday after the Friday autopsy.

“I am just asking for the body of my daughter to be released. The DCI informed us to plan for burial since the autopsy will be conducted on the eve of the funeral,” said the father.“We did not sanction the postmortem, and we are demanding the release of the body of my daughter,” Kangogo's father Barnaba Korir added.

A stand-off ensued at the funeral home after the family attempted to storm inside and forcefully remove the body.

“I have no issues. However, I don’t understand why they did not inform us that they would not facilitate the autopsy. Honestly, we could have postponed the funeral,” he said.

Caroline's brother, Mark Kangogo, accused the DCI of causing them emotional and psychological stress due to the unexpected change of events.

Johansen Oduor, the government's chief pathologist, claimed he was alerted late about the autopsy and that it could not have been performed on Friday, July 24.

Alfred Majimbo, the DCI chief in Elgeyo Marakwet, said they were awaiting directions from Nairobi headquarters.
