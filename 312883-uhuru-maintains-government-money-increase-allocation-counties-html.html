Uhuru maintains government has no money to increase allocation to counties

April 18, 2024
Post

- The head of state instead urged counties to utilise funds properly and find other ways of increasing their revenue collection

- Uhuru urged governors to accept the money that has been allocated to counties for Kenyans to continue receiving services

- The governors have been pushing for KSh 335 billion but the national government has only offered KSh 316 billion

President Uhuru Kenyatta has once again reiterated government has no more money to allocate counties.

Governors and senators have been pushing to have KSh 335 billion that the Commission on Revenue Allocation gave counties for the financial year 2019/2020.

Speaking on Wednesday, August 14, during meeting with Nakuru county MCAs, the president advised governors to find ways to improve their own revenue collection to finance counties.

"I plead to our houses of Parliament that we cannot give that which does not exist. If it increases we shall allocate them more, for now we just need to take what we have," he said.

So intense is the stalemate that a number of county workers have downed their tools over their July 2019 salaries which have been delayed.

“It is unfortunate that every day has turned out to a competition between the two houses while Kenyans are now suffering the brunt of this standoff. With all due respect, agree with me that there is money so that the little available funds can be released so that Kenyans can be served,” Uhuru said.

Governors and senators recently moved to court to protest the MPs' decision to allocate counties KSh 316 billion instead KSh 335 billion they were pushing for.

The disagreement emanated from the passage of the Division of Revenue Bill which is supposed to define how resources are shared between national and county governments.

Senators and MPs have locked horns on the right procedure to be followed in deliberating on the Bill before it is passed.

While senators insist they must be part of the process since it concerns counties, legislators maintain the draft is purely a money Bill and senators have no role in its legislation.
