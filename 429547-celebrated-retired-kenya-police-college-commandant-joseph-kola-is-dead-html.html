Joseph Kola: Retired Kenya Police College Commandant Dies, Kenyans Mourn

March 13, 2024
Post
  • The National Police Service mourned Joseph Kola as a great mentor who inspired many of his colleagues
  • Kola retired in the 1990s after holding several senior positions in the police service
  • Kenyans on social media urged NPS to improve the welfare of its officers

Celebrated retired Kenya Police College commandant Joseph Kola is dead.

Kola's death was confirmed by the National Police Service in a statement issued on Wednesday, October 13.

"The National Police Service cares for its retired officers. Therefore, we are saddened to learn of the passing on of Joseph Mudambi Kola, a retired deputy commissioner of police."The late Kola is remembered as a former commandant of Kenya Police College before moving to the then Rift Valley Province as a provincial police officer (PPO) where he retired in the early 90's, "NPS said.

The NPS said the deceased mentored many senior officers during his tenure in office.

He was described as someone who touched and impacted the lives of his colleagues.

Even as Kenyans mourned Kola, they called on NPS to improve officers' pay in the lower cadre.

TUKO.co.ke sampled some comments:

Muriithi Joe:

"The care and concern you claim to have should also be extended to junior officers."

Mutwiri Gitonga:

"Can you extend that care to junior officers!"

Monos Boyo:

"RIP. The military is the only organisation that cares for its retired officers, they give their retired medical insurance cover."

Adams:

"The care should also be shown even to retired constables/corporals. RIP."

Ndungu Wa Embu:

"Caring for them while at work is better."

Francis Boit's death

Kola's death comes a year after Rift Valley deputy regional commissioner Francis Boit passed on.

Boit had been unwell and was in and out of hospitals for health complications.

The medical condition of the heavily built officer worsened on the evening of Thursday, October 15, 2020, after which he was rushed to the hospital but took his last breath on the way.

The late Boit hit the headlines in 2018 when a photo of him and Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i went viral on social media platforms.

At that time, he was in Homa Bay county where he served as the deputy county commissioner.
