Kind police officers help homeless lady give birth on street on Christmas Day

June 14, 2024
Post

- The four officers were on patrol when they were alerted by a woman's distress

- A group of passersby directed them to where the woman was

- The pregnant lady could not wait for medical assistance forcing the officers to deliver the child

- They first helped bring the child to the world and then called for an ambulance

A group of Metro cops in South Africa went beyond the call of duty when they helped a homeless woman give birth on Christmas Day.

A homeless woman who was pregnant appeared to be in distress and the officers rushed to assist.

The four law enforcement officers discovered the woman giving birth on the pavement.

According to the Daily Sun, it was too late to call an ambulance, so they delivered the baby themselves.

The police officers were stopped by a group of homeless people who alerted them about a woman giving birth at the corner of Rissik and De Villiers streets in the CBD.

The officer: Sibusiso Lembede, Sibongiseni Dlame, Mduzuzi Ncongwane and Sydney Betha did not hesitate and immediately rushed to the scene.

It was too late to wait for medical assistance so they delivered the baby right there and waited for medical assistance.

An ambulance was called after the baby was safely delivered. Both the mum and the baby were transported to the Charlotte Maxeke Hospital.
