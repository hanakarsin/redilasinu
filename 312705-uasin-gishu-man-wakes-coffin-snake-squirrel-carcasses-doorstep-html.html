Uasin Gishu man wakes up to coffin with snake, squirrel carcasses at doorstep

March 15, 2024
Post

- Meshack Nyambati woke up on Monday to a small coffin at his doorstep

- He alerted his neighbours before calling police from Kiambaa Police Station

- Police officers opened the casket and found a dead squirrel, snake and an empty envelope

Police in Kapsaret, Uasin Gishu county, have commenced investigations into a case where a family woke up and found a coffin with carcasses of a snake and squirrel lying at their doorstep.

According to a police report seen by TUKO.co.ke, the casket which also had a white empty envelop is believed to have been placed at the doorstep of Meshack Nyambati on the night of Sunday, August 11.

"Meshack woke up on Monday, August 12, 2019, at around 6.30am  and upon opening his door,  he found a small coffin outside. The coffin had a dead snake, an empty envelope and a dead squirrel," said the police.

Upon seeing the casket which was barely a metre away from their doorstop, Nyambati alerted neighbours before reporting the matter to Kiambaa Police Station.

"It was so shocking. My family is now living in fear because we are yet to know the motive of dumping the coffin outside my house," he said.

He said police officers upon opening the casket were dazzled to discover it had some unusual contents.

"We are a Christian family and we have not wronged anyone to deserve this kind o treatment," Nyambati added.

Police investigating the scary incident said someone might have been out to throw the family into panic for reasons that are yet to be ascertained.

They vowed to bring the culprits to book.

Should police the find the suspect who dropped off the items at Nyambati’s house in what seemed like a death threat, he or she faces a prison sentence not exceeding 10 years.
