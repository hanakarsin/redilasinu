What is an OGA file?

Music files are stored in different formats. You are probably aware of MP3, one of the most common and highly utilized formats. However, an unfamiliar extension, OGA has been troubling a lot of people. What does it mean?

OGA audio files are associated with the Ogg Vorbis by Xiph.org Foundation. This firm focuses on providing free multimedia codecs and tools. It is an open and standardized bitstream container format that is purposefully created for streaming and manipulation. OGA is a compression file format for audio files.

The name, OGA represents “Ogg,” which is the name of the container, while “Vorbis" is the signature of the compression methodology. It is important to note that the audio file format is open-source and not patented.

Indeed, the term is foreign to many, but it is not uncommon to find audio files with this extension. Ordinarily, any media player ought to support this extension. However, some players might not, and you might want to convert OGA audio to MP3 online to listen to your music.
