Israel: Over 44 Worshippers Killed in Crush during Religious Gathering at Mount Meron

February 17, 2024
Post

- Israeli investigators are looking into what caused a crush at a mass religious congregation in Mount Meron that killed at least 44 worshippers

- Thousands of worshipers flocked to the mountain burial site to commemorate the Lag B'Omer holiday

- It is an annual celebration in which participants sing, dance and light fires in honour of Rabbi Shim Bar Yochai, a second-century Mishnaic sage

- The festival, however, turned chaotic when a massive wave of people trapped others underneath them, including children

At least 44 people have been killed and about 150 injured in a crowd crush at a Jewish religious gathering in northern Israel attended by tens of thousands of people.

Children were among the missing, according to Eli Beer, the director of Hatzalah, an Israeli volunteer ambulance service.

The wounded were rushed to hospitals across the country, according to the national ambulance service.

Among those rushed to the hospital were six people in critical condition and 18 people in stable condition.

The crowds at Mount Meron were estimated at 100,000.

Ultra-Orthodox Jews had travelled to the Galilee tomb of the second-century sage Rabbi Shimon Bar Yochai for annual Lag BaOmer commemorations.
